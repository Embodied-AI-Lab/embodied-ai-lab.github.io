# Lab Format & Logistics

## Format

The Embodied Intelligence Labs are designed to balance **concepts**, **implementation**,
and **reflection**.

Typical structure for each lab session:

- **Intro lecture / demo**  
  Short overview of the problem setting, safety considerations, and the tools
  you will use in the lab.

- **Guided implementation tasks**  
  Step-wise tasks with intermediate checkpoints so you can validate your design
  and code incrementally.

- **Experimentation and evaluation**  
  Run experiments, collect data, and compare different strategies or parameter
  settings for your agent or rover.

- **Discussion and reflection**  
  Brief reflection on failure cases, safety margins, and insights about
  intelligent and safe behavior in cyber-physical systems.

## Deliverables

The exact deliverables may vary by semester, but may include:

- **Technical report or lab notebook** summarizing:
  - design decisions,
  - safety requirements and constraints,
  - key results and observations.
- **Annotated code and configuration files** submitted via the course repository
  or the LMS.
- **Short demo or presentation** (depending on the offering) highlighting
  interesting scenarios, successes, and limitations.

## Logistics

Details such as:

- schedule of lab sessions,
- grading breakdown and rubrics,
- and links to starter code repositories

will be announced on the course LMS (e.g., Canvas) and referenced from this `mdBook`
as they become available.
