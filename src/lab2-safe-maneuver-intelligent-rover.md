# Lab 2: Safe Maneuver of Intelligent Rover

## Goal

In this lab, you will implement **safe motion planning and control** on an
**intelligent rover platform** with real-world sensing and actuation.

The focus is on ensuring that the rover:

- navigates its environment effectively, and  
- respects safety constraints such as collision avoidance and safe stopping.

## Focus

- Perception, planning, and control for mobile robots  
- Safety constraints for collision avoidance and safe stopping  
- Hands-on integration with a physical rover platform

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this lab, students should be able to:

- Integrate sensing, mapping, and control on a mobile robot
- Design safe maneuvering behaviors in constrained environments
- Analyze failure modes and safety margins under uncertainty

## Example components

Depending on the hardware/software stack used in a given semester, the lab may involve:

- A differential-drive or Ackermann-steering rover platform
- Obstacle detection using onboard sensors (e.g., lidar, depth camera, ultrasonic)
- Local planners that enforce minimum clearance and stopping distances

Implementation details, APIs for the rover, and safety guidelines will be provided
on the course LMS and/or linked from this `mdBook`.
